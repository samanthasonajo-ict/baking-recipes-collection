Classic Vanilla Cupcakes (with easy buttercream)

Introduction

Cupcakes are the perfect little celebration in one bite. These classic vanilla cupcakes are soft, moist, and reliably tasty — great for beginners and busy students who want a quick, crowd-pleasing dessert. The batter is simple and forgiving: you cream butter and sugar, add eggs and vanilla, then fold in dry ingredients with milk. After a quick bake, top with a simple buttercream and you have an attractive treat. I like these because they’re easy to portion, decorate, and pack for school events or picnics. They also offer endless variation possibilities: swap vanilla for lemon zest, fold in berries, or add cocoa for chocolate cupcakes.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup butter, softened
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a muffin tin with paper cups.
  2. Cream butter and sugar until light. Add eggs one at a time, then vanilla.
  3. Sift flour, baking powder, and salt. Alternate adding dry mix and milk to the butter mix; fold gently.
  4. Fill cups 2/3 full and bake 15–18 minutes or until a toothpick comes out clean.
  5. Cool 10 minutes in pan, transfer to rack. Prepare buttercream (butter + powdered sugar + milk + vanilla) and pipe on cooled cupcakes.
  6. Decorate as you like — sprinkles, zest, or a small fruit make nice finishing touches.
