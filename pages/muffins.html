Blueberry Muffins — quick and fluffy

Introduction

Muffins are the laid-back cousin of cupcakes—less sweet, more rustic, and perfect for breakfast or a snack. These blueberry muffins are tender and moist, with juicy bursts in every bite. The trick is not to overmix the batter: stir until ingredients are just combined so the muffins stay light. I love making a double batch to freeze; they reheat well and keep mornings easy. This recipe is forgiving: use fresh or frozen berries, and try variations with lemon zest or streusel topping. With simple pantry ingredients you can produce bakery-style muffins at home that feel special without fuss.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1/3 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 cup blueberries (fresh or frozen)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a muffin tin or grease cups.
  2. Whisk dry ingredients in a bowl. In another bowl, combine milk, oil, and egg.
  3. Pour wet into dry and stir until just combined; small lumps are OK. Fold in blueberries gently.
  4. Spoon batter into cups, fill almost full. Optionally sprinkle sugar on top for a crunchy finish.
  5. Bake 18–22 minutes until tops are golden and a toothpick comes out clean. Cool slightly and enjoy.
