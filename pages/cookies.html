Classic Chocolate Chip Cookies

Introduction

Chocolate chip cookies are the ultimate comfort food. They are quick to mix, easy to bake, and universally loved. This version balances chewy centers with crisp edges, thanks to a mix of white and brown sugar. I recommend chilling the dough for at least 30 minutes to deepen the flavor and prevent spreading. You can also customize them with nuts, dried fruit, or different types of chocolate. Fresh out of the oven, these cookies pair perfectly with a glass of milk or a cup of coffee. Keep a jar nearby, and they’ll disappear faster than you expect.

Ingredients

  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 cup butter, softened
  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • 3/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 cups chocolate chips

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Line baking sheets with parchment paper.
  2. Cream butter with both sugars until light. Beat in eggs and vanilla.
  3. Whisk together flour, baking soda, and salt. Gradually mix into butter mixture.
  4. Fold in chocolate chips. Chill dough 30 minutes for best texture.
  5. Drop tablespoon-sized scoops onto baking sheets. Bake 10–12 minutes until golden on edges.
  6. Cool slightly before transferring to a wire rack. Enjoy warm or store in an airtight container.
