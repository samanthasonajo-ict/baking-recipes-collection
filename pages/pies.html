Classic Apple Pie with Flaky Crust

Introduction

Apple pie is a timeless dessert that brings warmth and nostalgia. The key is a flaky, buttery crust and spiced apple filling that balances sweet and tart. This recipe focuses on technique: keep ingredients cold for the crust, use firm apples like Granny Smith for structure, and don’t overwork the dough. The filling cooks down to a syrupy texture while the crust browns to a beautiful golden color. Serve with vanilla ice cream for classic comfort. Making apple pie is a rewarding weekend project: the aroma itself fills the house and signals a special treat is near.

Ingredients

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour (for crust)
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 6 cups peeled and sliced apples
  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • 2 tbsp flour (for filling)
  • 1 tsp cinnamon, pinch of nutmeg
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice

Instructions

  1. Make crust: cut cold butter into flour and salt until pea-sized crumbs form. Add cold water a tablespoon at a time to form dough. Chill 1 hour.
  2. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Roll half the dough and line pie pan.
  3. Mix apples with sugar, flour, spices, and lemon. Fill crust, dot with butter, top with second crust or lattice.
  4. Brush with egg wash and bake 20 minutes at 400°F, then reduce to 350°F and bake another 25–30 minutes until filling bubbles and crust is golden.
  5. Cool before slicing to let filling set. Serve warm with ice cream if desired.
