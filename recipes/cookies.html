Classic Chocolate Chip Cookies

Chocolate chip cookies are perhaps the most popular cookie in the world, and for good reason. The balance of soft, chewy centers with slightly crispy edges makes them irresistible. The key ingredients are flour, sugar, butter, eggs, vanilla, and, of course, chocolate chips. Some bakers prefer to chill the dough before baking, which enhances the flavor and texture. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for about 10–12 minutes until the edges turn golden brown.

This recipe can be customized to your liking. For example, swap chocolate chips for white chocolate, or add chopped nuts for extra crunch. If you prefer a chewier cookie, use more brown sugar. For crispier cookies, bake a minute or two longer. No matter how you like them, these cookies are perfect with a glass of milk or as a sweet treat any time of the day.
